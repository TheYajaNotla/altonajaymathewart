01 // THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK

Artist Statement & Process Methodology

My practice explores the dimensional and affective capacity of colour and form, using the painted surface as a site where colour accumulates, compresses and destabilises[cite: 252]. Through metaboloid forms set within shifting frameworks, my work sustains an engagement with gesture, movement and memory, approaching abstraction as a mode of lived experience, adjuncted between pure formalism and emotion[cite: 253].

My practice investigates colour fields under tension, where the torque of the line activates the surface and sustains a continual negotiation[cite: 254]. I begin with a charcoal-based 'automatist' underdrawing, developing the composition through successive overlays of contrast-heavy hues[cite: 255]. Falcate forms emerge through contact and disarray, fraying into moments of chromatic stability[cite: 256]. The surface becomes a charged field capable of recording bodily presence, where hesitation and resolve are forces towards the deliberate density that emerges[cite: 257].

Compositional structures act as tools to push and pull between containment and release[cite: 258]. Lines function simultaneously as connective tissue and disruptive force, compressing space while revealing rhythmic pathways through the image[cite: 259]. Colour is imbued with agency; it acts as a sealant of time and a carrier of memory[cite: 260]. Repeated gestures leave residue, and each chromatic interaction tests the eye, unlocking a shifting and unstable reality sustained in a state of ludic restlessness[cite: 261].

Situated in response to the contemporary Indian condition, my work reflects an awareness of how multiplicity and saturation generates visual flux[cite: 262]. The paintings resist fixed resolution, holding space for ambiguity and transformation[cite: 263]. Through this evolving, process-driven practice, I navigate the gestural trace of presence, allowing the depth of the personal experience to reverberate with and against my socio-cultural milieu[cite: 264].

03 // BIOGRAPHICAL PROFILE

Interdisciplinary Context

Alton Ajay Mathew (b. 2000, Chennai, India) is an emerging contemporary abstract artist whose current practice examines saturation, containment and chromatic tension within constructed systems[cite: 266]. Working through charcoal-based underdrawings and successive overlays of high-contrast hues, his paintings investigate the resistance tethered between structure and emergence[cite: 267].

Rooted in a process-driven methodology, his work approaches abstraction as a site of lived experience, where gesture accumulates as human residue and colour functions as a carrier of bodily memory[cite: 268]. The surface becomes a charged field to examine the multiplicity of modern life and the tensions of negotiating an identity within its frameworks[cite: 269].

Alton has completed his medical degree from Poland in 2024, and brings an interdisciplinary perspective to his praxis, informed by a sensitivity to structural thinking and embodied awareness.

During this period, his early figurative and ceramics pieces were included in the 'Otwarta Wystawa' at the Book Art Museum, Łódź (2019)[cite: 271]. He has since engaged regularly in locally held life drawing sessions in Poland, and taken part in community art education initiatives conducted by 'Fundacja Arternatywa'[cite: 272]. He currently lives and works in the city of Chennai, India, maintaining an independent studio practice exploring painting with oil on canvas[cite: 273].
